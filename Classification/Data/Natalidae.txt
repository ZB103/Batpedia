*Location
Northern Mexico to southern Brazil, West Indies
*Habitat
These bats live in tropical lowlands and dry deciduous forests, roosting in caves and mines.
*Diet
Insectivorous
*Social Structure
They live within social groups ranging from 1 to 10,000 individuals. Not much else is known.
*Reproduction
Females gather in maternity colonies and give birth to a single offspring in the late dry season.
*Seasonal Behavior
(Unknown)
*Lifespan
(Unknown)
*Predators
Cats are their only known predator.
*Threats
Habitat loss threatens these bats.
*Status

*Appearance
These are small, long-legged bats with highly angular faces, large ears, and no noseleaf. Their fur can be brown, grey, yellow, or reddish. Their tail is enclosed in a well-developed uropatagium. Adult males have a natalid organ on the muzzle or face.
*Size
1.4-2.2 in (3.5-5.5 cm)
