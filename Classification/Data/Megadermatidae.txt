*Location
South and southeast Asia, Indonesia, Philippines, central Africa, northern Australia
*Habitat
Tropical, subtropical
*Diet
Primarily insectivorous, occasionally carnivorous, occasionally cannibalistic
Carnivorous (Macroderma gigas)
*Social Structure
Roosting occurs in caves, rock crevaces, buildings, and trees, and roost in groups of 1-30. These bats "sing" to defend their foraging territory from other bats.
Uniquely, some of these bats are monogamous. In Lyroderma lyra, the females segregate themselves after mating.

*Appearance
Medium to large bats, brown, white, gray, green, or yellow in color, with large eyes, long, erect noseleaves and huge, oval ears joined at the base by skin across the forehead. They have a large tail membrane, but a short or absent tail. Their wings tend to be short and broad.
